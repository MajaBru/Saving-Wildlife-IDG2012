FOCUS: The Norwegian wolf controversy

In Norway, the wolf is listed on the red list as "critically endangered". In the winter of 2016/2017, 105-112 wolves were detected with full or partial residence on Norwegian land - 54-56 who only stayed within the land border, and 51-56 who lived on the Norwegian and Swedish sides. But the number of wolves varies greatly mortality, and may suddenly be down again to 30-40 wolves on the Norwegian side.

The wolf plays a significant role in the dynamics of the northern ecosystems. Behavioral patterns in reindeer, bison, musk deer, elk and a number of other game species reflect an evolutionary adaptation to wolves. The Scandinavian ecosystems have in practice been without wolves for 100 years, and have developed completely differently than they would have done with wolves present.
