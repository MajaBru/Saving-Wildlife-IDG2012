Our work

The situation

Norway's wild animals and birds are basically not owned by anyone, and protected unless otherwise determined by law. Unfortunately, the law allows large-scale exploitation of them - also of the many species that are now on the Norwegian red list of threatened species. Whether it's recreational hunting or "damage" because wild animals are a "nuisance" or "inconvenience" to people - many species are victims of persecution.

Our engangement

Already in the earliest work of Saving Wildlife, respect for the wild animals and their natural environment had a central place. But the fight for them has become increasingly more important to us over the past few years.

Saving Wildlife has engaged in wildlife regulations as well as the principled debate on attitudes. There are many "grey areas" in the regulations surrounding hunting, and we have worked to shed light on these and change the rules in favor of the animals. Hunting training for animals in captivity, release of animals for hunting, "catch and release" of fish and shot prizes are some issues
